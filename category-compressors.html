Critical Starting Air Solutions

The main air compressor is critical for vessel maneuverability. A failure in the starting air system can leave a vessel dead in the water. At Universal Exports, we specialize in reconditioning multi-stage high-pressure compressors (up to 30-40 Bar) used for main engine starting.

We stock popular models like the Tanabe H-Series (H-63, H-64, H-273) and Sperre HL-Series. Our workshop is equipped to handle the complete refurbishment of these reciprocating machines, ensuring they can build pressure quickly and hold it without leaking.

Our 5-Step Reconditioning Process

  • 1. Block & Cylinder Inspection: The compressor block is checked for cracks. Cylinders are measured for ovality and honed to remove glazing, ensuring proper ring seating.
  • 2. Valve Refurbishment: Suction and discharge valves are the most common failure points. We disassemble, clean, and lap the valve plates. Springs are replaced with new ones.
  • 3. Piston & Ring Replacement: Pistons are cleaned and inspected. Piston rings are almost always replaced with new OEM-spec rings to ensure compression efficiency.
  • 4. Cooler Cleaning: Intercoolers and aftercoolers are chemically cleaned to remove scale and carbon buildup, ensuring efficient heat transfer and preventing overheating.
  • 5. Final Pressure Test: The assembled unit is run on our test bed. We verify the time taken to fill a standard air receiver from 0 to 30 Bar to ensure it meets factory specs.

"We don't just check if it runs. We check how fast it pumps. Efficiency is key."
