Restoring Hydraulic Efficiency

Hydraulic systems on ships operate in the harshest environments. Saltwater corrosion and contaminated oil can destroy pumps and motors. At Universal Exports, we specialize in the refurbishment of large-capacity hydraulic units used in deck machinery (cranes, windlasses) and steering gear.

We source our cores from vessels being recycled at Alang, focusing on high-value units like Fukushima gear pumps and IHI vane pumps. Our workshop is equipped with a custom-built hydraulic test bench capable of simulating full load conditions up to 500 Bar.

Our Refurbishment Process

  • 1. Disassembly & Inspection: The unit is completely stripped. We inspect the cam ring, rotor, and vanes (for vane pumps) or the cylinder block and pistons (for axial piston pumps) for scoring and wear.
  • 2. Lapping & Grinding: Critical surfaces like the valve plate and cylinder block face are lapped to optical flatness to ensure a perfect seal and maintain volumetric efficiency.
  • 3. Seal Replacement: All O-rings, oil seals, and gaskets are replaced with high-temperature Viton or NBR kits to prevent external leaks.
  • 4. Bearing Replacement: Shaft bearings are replaced with premium SKF/FAG/NTN bearings to handle the radial and axial loads.
  • 5. Load Testing: The reassembled pump is mounted on our test bench. We run it for 2-4 hours, monitoring flow rate, pressure build-up, case drain temperature, and noise levels.

"A pump that can't hold pressure is useless. We verify performance under load before it leaves our shop."
